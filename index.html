Berlin: The Capital of Fashion, Nightlife, and Culture in Germany

The city of Berlin, also known is the capital of Germany, hosts about 3.85 million people, yet is one of the smallest states in the country.

Gif of a city skyline above water with the sun moving in the background.

Fashion

If the idea of haute couture or inventive style resonates with you, you'll love the fashion scene in Berlin. With the plethora of inventive minds and creative souls in the city, just walking the streets of its upscale district, Charlottenburg, is sure to inspire you.

*Fun Fact:* Berlin's most fashionable people are often stereotyped to wear sunglasses much more often and unnecessarily than usual. After all, they do say the eyes are the window to the soul; so that begs the question: what are Berliners hiding?
Image of two fashionable women walking through Berlin.

Nightlife

If your interests happen to be similar to mine, you'd most definitely enjoy the club scene in Berlin. Known to have the best club scene in Europe, Berlin hosts some of the most infamous, enthralling, and riveting nightclubs. Berghain is a great place to go, of course as long as you're of age and of style. People have been known to line up for hours just to get in, even with the very real chance of being denied entry.

*Fun Fact:* Club Berghain either simply annoyed or denied entry to Elon Musk due to their "PEACE" sign outdoors, which resulted in him not being able to get into the infamous nightclub.
Image of the empty, yet esoteric structure of the inside of the Berghain club.

Culture

Berlin's culture surrounds mostly art, so if you consider yourself to love the arts (you likely do if you've made it this far reading my magnificent website) you'll have plenty to do if and when you visit. Hosting around 180 museums and 440 galleries, Berlin boasts a plethora of artistic outlets.

*Fun Fact:* Berlin's Museumsinsel is the largest museum campus in the world, and also happens to be a UNESCO World Heritage site.
